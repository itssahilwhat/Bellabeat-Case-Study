Bellabeat Data Analysis

Introduction

Bellabeat is a health-focused company founded in 2013 that manufactures smart wellness products for women. Their products and app collect data on activity, stress, sleep, and health, empowering women to make informed decisions about their wellness. With its growing potential, Bellabeat aims to expand its presence in the smart device market. Since 2016, Bellabeat has marketed its products through digital channels like Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, Twitter, and Google Search.

Business Task

Bellabeats goal is to analyze how users interact with non-Bellabeat smart devices. The insights will help inform strategies to improve Bellabeats marketing and customer engagement. The specific questions to address are:

  1. What are the current trends in smart device usage?
  2. How can these trends benefit Bellabeat customers?
  3. How can these trends improve Bellabeats marketing strategy?

Step 2: Prepare

Data Source

For this analysis, I used the following public dataset: FitBit Fitness Tracker Data (CC0: Public Domain, dataset made available through Mobius).

This dataset contains personal health data from 30 Fitbit users, who consented to submit their personal tracker data, including minute-level output for physical activity, heart rate, and sleep monitoring. It includes data about daily activity, steps, and heart rate that can be used to explore users habits.

Key Details: - A total of 18 CSV files were downloaded and stored properly for this analysis. - The dataset includes various metrics that provide insights into daily activity, sleep patterns, and health tracking.

ROCCC Analysis

The ROCCC framework was applied to evaluate the dataset:

  • Reliable: Low The data was collected from users without demographic information, which might affect its reliability.
  • Originality: Low The data was collected from third-party Amazon Mechanical Turk users, limiting its originality.
  • Comprehensive: High The dataset contains a broad range of personal health metrics, enabling the answering of business questions.
  • Current: Low The data was collected between 03.12.201605.12.2016, making it outdated for current trend analysis.
  • Cited: High The dataset source is well-documented and publicly available on Kaggle.

Guiding Questions

1. Where is your data stored?

The data is stored on Kaggle, a popular platform for sharing datasets. It can be downloaded from the FitBit Fitness Tracker Data page to local machines or cloud storage.

2. How is the data organized?

The data is organized in wide format, where: - Each column represents a specific feature (e.g., steps, heart_rate, activity_level). - Each row represents a time-series data entry (e.g., daily or minute-level data for a user).

3. Are there issues with bias or credibility in this data?

Yes, there are some concerns: - Bias: The dataset may be biased toward health-conscious individuals or Fitbit users, limiting its generalizability. - Credibility: While the data is from Fitbit users, it was collected through Amazon Mechanical Turk, which may affect data consistency.

ROCCC Summary: - Reliable: Low - Original: Low - Comprehensive: High - Current: Low - Cited: High

4. How are you addressing licensing, privacy, security, and accessibility?

  • Licensing: The dataset is shared under the CC0: Public Domain license, meaning it can be freely used without restrictions.
  • Privacy: The data does not contain personally identifiable information. Users provided their data anonymously with consent.
  • Security: The dataset is securely stored locally or in encrypted cloud storage.
  • Accessibility: The dataset is freely accessible on Kaggle, ensuring transparency and ease of use.

5. How did you verify the datas integrity?

The datasets integrity was verified through: - Checking the source credibility (Kaggle and Mobius). - Ensuring there are no missing or inconsistent values. - Identifying and removing duplicates. - Validating the plausibility of values (e.g., no negative step counts or unrealistic heart rate values).

6. How does it help you answer your question?

This dataset provides detailed insights into user activity, sleep, and heart rate, which align closely with Bellabeats products and target market. By analyzing this data, trends in smart device usage can be identified, enabling Bellabeat to improve its products and marketing strategies.

7. Are there any problems with the data?

Yes, there are potential issues: - Missing or incomplete data: Some users might not have consistently worn their devices, leading to gaps. - Bias: The dataset represents only Fitbit users, which may not generalize to Bellabeats broader target audience. - Outdated data: The dataset is several years old and may not reflect current trends in device usage.

Deliverable

Supporting visualizations and key findings.

Follow these steps:

  1. Take out a piece of paper and a pen and sketch some ideas for how you will visualize the data.
  2. Once you choose a visual form, open your tool of choice to create your visualization. Use a presentation software, such as PowerPoint or Google Slides; your spreadsheet program; Tableau; or R.
  3. Create your data visualization, remembering that contrast should be used to draw your audiences attention to the most important insights. Use artistic principles including size, color, and shape.
  4. Ensure clear meaning through the proper use of common elements, such as headlines, subtitles, and labels.
  5. Refine your data visualization by applying deep attention to detail.
